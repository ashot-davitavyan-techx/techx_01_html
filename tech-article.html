:( Microsoft’s ‘Blue Screen of Death’ Is Going Away

Author: Ashot Davitavyan

The blue screen that stressed computer users for more than three decades is giving way to a black one.

For years, the "Blue Screen of Death" (BSOD) was a symbol of frustration for Windows users.

Appearing without warning, it indicated a serious system error or crash, often requiring a reboot and sometimes leading to lost work.

With the release of Windows 11, Microsoft has decided to change the iconic error screen’s color from blue to black, aligning it with the new startup and shutdown screens, which also feature a sleek black background.
